Problem: Let \(k\geq14\) be an integer, and let \(p_k\) be the largest prime number which is strictly less than \(k\). You may assume that \(p_k\geq3k/4\). Let \(n\) be a composite integer. Prove:
(a) if \(n=2p_k\), then \(n\) does not divide \((n-k)!\);
(b) if \(n>2p_k\), then \(n\) divides \((n-k)!\).
